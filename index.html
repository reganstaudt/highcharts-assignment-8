United States Hate Crime Statistics: 2019

By: Regan Staudt

2019 Hate Crime Incident Classifications

The word “hate” is thrown around in our society. I do not take the word "hate" lightly. Hate is feeling so violent that you can only describe it with that word. In 2019 the concept of hate resulted in 7,314 incidents. Hate crime is defined as "a crime, typically one involving violence, that is motivated by prejudice on the basis of ethnicity, religion, sexual orientation, or similar grounds." Over 7,000 incidents of this malice. The United States dictates that if a crime committed has been fueled with prejudice precursors then said offence could be deemed as a hate crime. Hate crimes do not only violate the victim, but also the community to which the victim belongs.

Hate Crimes 2019: Race

Sadly, racial hate crimes have proliferated through the media for decades. In 2020, this really came to light. With the murder of George Floyd the country errupted into protest. People wanted to justice for this hate crime. As the chart up top shows, race is the number one motivation for hate crimes. Within race, crimes against African-Americans account for almost half of all race indunced hate crimes.
